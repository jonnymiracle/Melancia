Size Guide

Every body is different. Use this guide to find your best Melancia fit — we want you to feel confident from the moment you put it on.

How to Measure for Tops

1

Bust

Measure around the fullest part of your chest, keeping the tape parallel to the ground. Breathe normally — don't pull the tape too tight.

2

Band / Underbust

Measure directly under your bust, where a bra band would sit. Keep the tape snug but comfortable.

3

Waist

Measure around your natural waist — the narrowest part of your torso, usually just above your belly button.

Tip: Use a soft measuring tape and wear minimal or no clothing. Take measurements in front of a mirror to keep the tape level.
Size Bust Band / Underbust Waist Cup (approx.)
XS 32–33" 27–28" 25–26" A/B
S 34–35" 29–30" 27–28" B/C
M 36–37" 31–32" 29–30" C/D
L 38–40" 33–35" 31–33" D/DD

Between sizes?

  • If you're between XS and S, size up for more coverage
  • If you're between S and M, size down for a more supportive fit
  • For fuller busts, size up one for tops and down for bottoms
  • Triangle tops adjust via ties — most flexible for sizing

Style Notes

  • Bandeau styles fit tighter — size up if unsure
  • Halter tops are adjustable at the neck and back
  • Our padding is removable on all padded styles
  • All tops are designed to stay secure in the water

How to Measure for Bottoms

1

Waist

Measure around your natural waist — the narrowest part, usually 1–2 inches above your belly button. Keep the tape relaxed, not pulled tight.

2

Hips

Stand with feet together and measure around the fullest part of your hips and seat, usually about 8–9 inches below your natural waist.

Tip: For tie-side bottoms, the ties give extra flexibility — these work well for all hips. For Brazilian cut, we recommend sizing up if you prefer more coverage.
Size Waist Hips US Jean Size (approx.)
XS 24–25" 34–35" 0 – 2
S 26–27" 36–37" 4 – 6
M 28–30" 38–40" 8 – 10
L 31–33" 41–43" 12 – 14

Coverage Guide

  • Brazilian cut — minimal coverage, cheeky fit
  • Classic cut — moderate coverage, sits at hip
  • High-waist — full coverage, elongates legs
  • Tie-side — adjustable; great for all hip sizes

Fit Tips

  • Size up if you prefer fuller coverage in the back
  • High-waist styles run true to size
  • Brazilian styles can run small — size up if unsure
  • Fabric has light stretch — fits snugly but comfortably

How to Measure for One Pieces

1

Bust

Measure around the fullest part of your chest, keeping the tape parallel to the ground.

2

Waist

Measure your natural waist — the narrowest point of your torso, above your belly button.

3

Hips

Measure around the fullest part of your hips and seat with feet together.

4

Torso Length

Measure from your shoulder (at the neck) straight down to your crotch. This is key for one-pieces to avoid pulling.

Tip: For one-pieces, torso length matters most. If you have a long torso, size up. If short, size down or look for adjustable strap styles.
Size Bust Waist Hips Torso Length
XS 32–33" 24–25" 34–35" 25–26"
S 34–35" 26–27" 36–37" 26–27"
M 36–37" 28–30" 38–40" 27–28"
L 38–40" 31–33" 41–43" 28–30"

One Piece Tips

  • Torso length is the most important measurement
  • Long torso? Size up or choose adjustable-strap styles
  • Short torso? Size down or choose styles with ruching
  • Plunge styles add visual length to the torso

Fit Check

  • Straps should lie flat without digging in
  • The crotch lining should sit flush — no pulling
  • Sides should be smooth with no gaping
  • You should be able to move freely — sit, bend, dive
